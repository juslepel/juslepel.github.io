Centre for Human Drug Research

The Centre for Human Drug Research tests new drug treatments, in particular for Central Nervous Systems. The company develops new methods to test, and collect and evaluate data for, these new drug methods. The company is currently developing a portable version of an already existing inhouse testing platform.
As a medior software engineer at this company, you will be designing and implementing the software solution for this project. The company asks for experience with Java, JavaScript, HTML and Agile development methods, together with general experience in the field and experience in architecture and design of cloudbased/hybrid software. The company describes itself as a pleasant workspace with an open work environment.
The environment definitely interests me. Healthcare has always interested me, I've even considered a nursing HBO study in the past. Even though you're not directly doing anything medical, you are contributing to it and connected to it. My parents have both had similar jobs, being in the healthcare field but not "at the front line".

QuTech

QuTech is a research institute at the TU Delft. They're located on the campus of the university, as well. They develope prototypes of a quantum computer and secure quantum internet. As a software engineer here you will be part of the Software Development Support team. The job entails maintaining the institute's software, and providing technical and non-technical support on software development. The position requires experience with both Windows and Linux, fluency in object-oriented programming, and a variety of other requests.
This would be a hybrid job, so time would be divided between home and the TU Delft campus. The team you would be a part of consist of 2-3 other people.
It seems interesting to work at a research centre. They also focus a lot on the fact that they are part of the university. Out of the 3 companies, I think that this opening and company would fit with me most.
